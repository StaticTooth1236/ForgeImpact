# Risk & Schedule Impact Brief

**Date:** 2026-07-12 13:21

**Proposed Change:** Reduce LRIP-2 production rate target from 6 to 4 units per year

## Risk & Schedule Impact Brief

**Proposed Change:**  
Reduce LRIP-2 production rate target from 6 to 4 units per year (33% reduction), maintaining or extending the 12-month phase duration planned for 2027.

**Overall Schedule Impact Level:** **High** – Creates significant downstream schedule compression risk at LRIP-3 transition, transforming a planned 2x rate increase into a 3x jump that threatens FRP readiness timeline.

**Key Milestones Affected:**
- **LRIP-2 Complete (Q4 2027):** Potential 6-month slip to Q2 2028 if phase extended to maintain learning curve
- **LRIP-3 Start (Q1 2028):** At high risk of delay due to inadequate preparation for 3x rate increase
- **LRIP-3 Complete (Q4 2028):** Cascading 6-month delay likely if LRIP-2 extends
- **LRIP-3 Gate Success Criteria:** MRL-8 achievement jeopardized – requires 3 months stable production at target rate, now with unproven 3x scaling
- **FRP Readiness Review (Q1 2029):** Could slip to Q3 2029, delaying MRL-9 certification and IOC timeline
- **Phase 2 Facility Complete (Q2 2029):** Timeline unchanged but lacks adequate production validation data

**Critical Path & Buffer Impact:**
- **Critical Path:** LRIP-3 transition becomes the new critical path constraint; the workforce ramp (240→450 personnel, 87% increase vs. planned 61%) and supplier capacity validation for 3x jump now drive schedule
- **Buffer Consumption:** LRIP-2's 3-month schedule reserve remains adequate if maintaining 12-month duration; however, **LRIP-3's 3-month buffer is insufficient** for a 3x rate increase (recommend increasing to 4-5 months)
- **Learning Curve Disruption:** 33% fewer builds during LRIP-2 slows takt time improvements (Forward Fuselage: 45→28 days target now at risk; Final Assembly: 65→45 days may not achieve), consuming hidden schedule margin in downstream phases
- **Phase Transition Risk:** Previously validated 2x rate increase success criterion (<15% variance) is now invalidated; 3x increase has no historical program data supporting feasibility within existing timeline

**Risk Profile Changes:**

*New Risks Created:*
- **R-NEW-001: Learning Curve Disruption** (Prob: High | Impact: High-Financial) – 12% cost reduction target from LRIP-1 to LRIP-2 unlikely; threatens 58% cost reduction by FRP-2, potentially adding $45-60M in unplanned costs
- **R-NEW-002: LRIP-3 Rate Jump Failure** (Prob: Medium-High | Impact: Critical-Schedule) – 3x rate increase unprecedented in program plan; high probability of 3-6 month delay to LRIP-3 start and potential IOC slip
- **R-NEW-003: Supplier Capacity Mismatch** (Prob: Medium | Impact: High) – Propulsion supplier's $85M capacity expansion planned for 2x increase; may be inadequately scoped or timed for 3x jump, requiring accelerated commitment (Q4 2026 vs. Q2 2027)
- **R-NEW-004: Facility Underutilization** (Prob: High | Impact: Medium-Cost) – Second assembly flow operates at 50% utilization vs. 75% planned; $15-20M in idle capital with workforce retention risk

*Existing Risks Elevated:*
- **Workforce Retention/Ramp Risk:** Elevated from Medium to High – idleness during LRIP-2 increases turnover risk, then requires 87% headcount increase (vs. 61% planned) in compressed 9-month window
- **Supplier Lead Time Risk:** Elevated from Low to Medium – 24-month supplier capacity expansions may be under-scoped for 3x transition
- **Gate Criteria Achievement Risk:** LRIP-3 gate (MRL-8) elevated to High – cannot demonstrate stable production at target rate with insufficient build repetitions

*Risks Reduced:*
- **LRIP-2 Execution Risk:** Reduced from Medium to Low – lower production volume decreases probability of quality escapes or delivery misses during this specific phase

*Overall Risk Posture:* **Significantly degraded** – Program shifts from moderate, managed risk profile to high-risk posture with critical path exposure at LRIP-3 transition. The change trades short-term execution certainty for long-term program schedule and cost risk.

**Manufacturing Ramp Implications:**
- **LRIP-2 Capacity Utilization:** Drops to 50% (4 units vs. 8-unit capacity across 2 flows); creates $15-20M stranded capital investment and workforce underutilization
- **Learning Curve Trajectory:** 38% takt time improvement targets (e.g., Forward Fuselage 45→28 days) require repetition cadence; reduced volume extends learning timeline by estimated 3-4 months, pushing achievement into LRIP-3
- **LRIP-3 Transition Complexity:** Transforms from proven 2x rate model to unvalidated 3x increase (4→12 units/year); historical aerospace program data shows >25% schedule variance on rate increases >2.5x
- **Workforce Ramp Steepness:** Hiring/training acceleration required – must start workforce build 12 months before LRIP-3 (vs. 9 months planned) to achieve 87% headcount increase; training pipeline capacity becomes constraint
- **Supplier Synchronization:** Long-lead suppliers (propulsion: 24 months, avionics: 18 months) face mismatch between LRIP-2 validation volume and LRIP-3 requirements; insufficient production data to validate scalability claims
- **FRP Readiness:** MRL-9 certification requires demonstrated scalability; 3x single-step increase does not satisfy "progressive rate maturation" criterion, potentially adding 3-6 month validation period before FRP declaration

**Recommendations & Mitigations:**

*Schedule Protection Measures:*
1. **Maintain 12-month LRIP-2 duration** – DO NOT extend beyond Q4 2027; absorb rate reduction through underutilization rather than phase extension to preserve downstream milestones
2. **Add 3-month buffer to LRIP-3** – Increase phase reserve from 3 to 5 months (total 8 months) to accommodate 3x transition uncertainty
3. **Advance LRIP-3 preparations** – Move workforce hiring start from T-9 months to T-12 months; initiate supplier capacity validation reviews in Q4 2026 (6 months early)
4. **Implement progressive rate-up within LRIP-2** – Structure as 2→3→4 unit quarterly pattern rather than flat 4/year to preserve learning momentum and provide partial validation of acceleration capability

*Risk Mitigation Actions:*
1. **Maintain workforce training tempo** – Continue full training/certification schedule for 280-person workforce despite only needing 220-240; use excess capacity for cross-training and process improvement
2. **Add process validation builds** – Utilize spare assembly flow capacity for additional test articles, pathfinder units, or parallel development hardware to preserve learning curve
3. **Accelerate supplier engagement** – Validate propulsion supplier's $85M expansion plan supports 3x jump; may require earlier contract commitment with risk-sharing provisions
4. **Defer selective capital** – Postpone $15-20M in second-flow tooling to 2028, but **maintain** Phase 1 facility timeline (required for LRIP-3 regardless of LRIP-2 volume)

*Gate Criteria Revisions:*
1. **Modify LRIP-3 Gate success criterion** – Revise from "<15% variance on 2x increase" to "<20% variance on 3x increase" with added requirement for demonstrated acceleration curve within LRIP-2
2. **Add interim validation gate** – Insert Q2 2027 checkpoint requiring demonstration of 3-unit quarterly rate and workforce productivity metrics on track
3. **Strengthen supplier readiness criterion** – Require signed capacity validation from all long-lead suppliers (propulsion, avionics, composites) confirming 12-unit/year capability by Q4 2027

*Decision Recommendation:*
**APPROVE WITH CONDITIONS** – The rate reduction is acceptable only if:
- LRIP-2 phase duration remains at 12 months (no extension beyond Q4 2027)
- $8-10M invested in learning curve preservation measures (additional test builds, enhanced training, process optimization)
- LRIP-3 schedule buffer increased to 5 months
- Workforce and supplier engagement timelines advanced by 3 months
- Gate criteria revised to reflect 3x transition reality

**Alternative:** Reject and propose LRIP-2 rate of 5 units/year (compromise) to limit LRIP-3 transition to 2.4x increase, which falls within industry-proven scalability range.
