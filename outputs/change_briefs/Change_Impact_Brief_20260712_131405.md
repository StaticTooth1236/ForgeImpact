# Change Impact Brief

**Date:** 2026-07-12 13:14

**Proposed Change:** Delay the second autoclave procurement by 4 months

## Change Impact Brief

**Proposed Change:**  
Delay the procurement of the second autoclave by 4 months from its currently planned acquisition date.

**Overall Impact Level:** **Medium** - Manageable schedule and production impacts, but creates risk to LRIP-2 transition timing and requires capital budget reallocation.

**Affected Areas:**
- Manufacturing capacity ramp timeline
- LRIP-1 to LRIP-2 transition schedule
- Capital Investment Plan (FY phasing)
- Production equipment installation schedule
- Manufacturing risk profile
- Annual production capacity targets

**Impact Summary:**  
Delaying the second autoclave procurement by 4 months will push equipment installation and checkout into late 2027 or early 2028, potentially impacting the facility expansion critical path for LRIP-2 transition. While LRIP-1 production (2 units/year) can proceed with single autoclave operation, the delay compresses schedule margin for achieving the 6 units/year LRIP-2 capacity target. The change offers short-term capital expenditure relief by shifting approximately $2.5-4M from one fiscal year to the next, but increases manufacturing ramp risk. Given the 40-week procurement timeline plus installation/checkout duration, recovery options are limited if further delays occur.

**Detailed Impacts:**

- **Configuration / Drawing Tree:** 
Minimal direct impact. No engineering drawings require revision. Manufacturing facility layout drawings and equipment installation plans will need date updates. The Manufacturing Ramp Plan (Section 3.0 Facility and Equipment Requirements) requires formal revision to reflect new autoclave #2 procurement and installation dates. Equipment installation sequence documentation needs timeline adjustment.

- **Bill of Materials & Supply Chain:** 
Low direct impact to aircraft BOM. The autoclave itself is capital equipment, not part of aircraft structure. However, the 4-month delay extends vendor lead time risk exposure - if composite material suppliers or autoclave manufacturer experience disruptions during the延 delay period, recovery becomes more difficult. Supplier agreements for long-lead composite materials may need renegotiation if production rate assumptions change. Risk of supplier capacity being allocated to competing programs during delay window.

- **Manufacturing & Production:** 
**Moderate to High impact.** Single autoclave operations remain viable for LRIP-1 (2 units/year) but eliminate redundancy and reduce schedule flexibility. Any unplanned autoclave downtime during LRIP-1 directly impacts delivery schedule with no backup capacity. The delay compresses the equipment commissioning window before LRIP-2 ramp (6 units/year target). Installation and checkout activities (Section 4.2.4 of IMS) will shift into Q3-Q4 2027 or later, overlapping with planned Phase 1 facility expansion activities, creating potential facility access conflicts and resource contention. Reduces ability to run parallel cure cycles, limiting process optimization and workforce training opportunities prior to rate increase.

- **Schedule (IMS):** 
**Medium impact with critical path risk.** The 40-week procurement cycle (Section 7.0, Phase 4.2.3) plus estimated 12-16 weeks for installation and checkout means 4-month delay could push autoclave #2 operational readiness from approximately Q2 2027 to Q4 2027 or Q1 2028. This directly threatens the LRIP-1 to LRIP-2 transition timing (currently planned Q3 2027 facility expansion per Section 2.2). The Manufacturing Ramp Plan identifies facility expansion as critical path item. While Section 8.2 indicates schedule risk buffers exist, a 4-month equipment delay consumes significant buffer and may push into the transition window. If LRIP-2 rate commitments are contractually obligated, this creates schedule compliance risk.

- **Cost & TCO:** 
**Short-term savings, long-term neutral to negative.** Immediate benefit: shifts $2.5-4M capital expenditure from current FY to next FY, providing budget relief (Section 6.0 Capital Investment Plan annual phasing). However, potential cost impacts include: (1) Price escalation risk - 4-month delay exposes procurement to potential vendor price increases or steel/material cost inflation; (2) Expedite costs if schedule compression becomes necessary later; (3) Potential inefficiency costs from single-autoclave operations (overtime, queue time, reduced optimization); (4) Risk of penalty costs if LRIP-2 delivery commitments are missed. Total Cost of Ownership may increase if production inefficiencies during extended single-autoclave period reduce overall throughput or increase scrap rates.

- **Risks:** 
**Increases multiple risk categories.** Creates or elevates: (1) **Manufacturing Capacity Risk** - eliminates autoclave redundancy during critical LRIP-1 period; (2) **Schedule Risk** - consumes buffer and tightens LRIP-2 transition timeline; (3) **Single Point of Failure** - any autoclave #1 failure during 4-month extension halts all composite production; (4) **Supply Chain Risk** - extends window for vendor issues to impact program; (5) **Workforce Development Risk** - delays dual-autoclave training and process optimization. Partially mitigates **Capital Cash Flow Risk** in near term. Recommend updating Risk Register with new/elevated manufacturing risks and revised probability/impact scores for existing facility expansion risks.

- **Certification & Compliance:** 
**Low direct impact.** No airworthiness certification impacts anticipated - autoclave procurement timing doesn't affect aircraft compliance. However, if the delay causes LRIP-2 schedule compression, this could create pressure to accelerate manufacturing processes in ways that increase quality risk. Production certificate requirements for rate increases should be reviewed to ensure autoclave #2 commissioning and qualification can still be completed before LRIP-2 production begins. Any contractual delivery commitments (government or commercial) need compliance review.

**Recommendations & Mitigations:**

1. **CONDITIONAL APPROVAL with mandatory mitigations:** The change can be approved only if the following conditions are implemented:

2. **Immediate Actions:**
   - Formalize autoclave #1 preventive maintenance program with enhanced inspection frequency and backup component inventory
   - Negotiate contract options with autoclave vendor to preserve price and allow acceleration if needed (pay small option fee to maintain 2-month pull-in capability)
   - Establish backup composite curing arrangements (partnerships with nearby aerospace facilities) for emergency use

3. **Schedule Mitigations:**
   - Formally reassess LRIP-2 transition date - if contractually flexible, consider 2-month slip to preserve margin
   - Fast-track facility preparation work for autoclave #2 installation to compress installation phase when equipment arrives
   - Develop parallel procurement documentation now to enable immediate release when delay period expires

4. **Manufacturing Planning:**
   - Optimize single-autoclave production schedule to maximize efficiency and minimize bottlenecks
   - Front-load workforce training and process optimization activities that can occur with one autoclave
   - Create contingency production schedule showing recovery paths if delays extend

5. **Risk Management:**
   - Elevate this decision to program leadership with clear visibility of reduced schedule margin
   - Update Risk Register and establish monthly monitoring of autoclave-related manufacturing risks
   - Establish decision gate at delay +2 months to reassess and potentially accelerate procurement

6. **Financial Tracking:**
   - Ringfence the delayed capital funds to prevent reallocation - ensure money remains available in next FY
   - Track any cost deltas (escalation, expedite potential) for informed decision-making

**Do NOT approve if:** LRIP-2 transition timing is contractually fixed with penalties, or if LRIP-1 already experiencing production delays.
