## Executive Summary

This Manufacturing Rate Ramp-up Plan establishes the strategic approach for scaling production of the Eurus Systems MAAP-1 (Modular Advanced Aerospace Platform) from initial low-rate production through full-rate production capability. The plan spans a 7-year period (2025-2031) and defines the phased approach to achieving a sustained production rate of 48 units per year by 2030.

**Key Milestones:**
- **LRIP Phase 1:** 2 units/year (2025-2026)
- **LRIP Phase 2:** 6 units/year (2027)
- **LRIP Phase 3:** 12 units/year (2028)
- **FRP Phase 1:** 24 units/year (2029)
- **FRP Phase 2:** 48 units/year (2030-2031+)

**Total Program Delivery:** 230+ units over production life cycle

## 1. Program Overview and Context

### 1.1 Program Background

The MAAP-1 program represents a next-generation aerospace platform incorporating advanced composite structures, integrated avionics systems, and modular mission-configurable architecture. The manufacturing strategy must balance:

- Customer delivery requirements
- Technology maturation and producibility improvements
- Capital investment phasing
- Workforce development timeline
- Supply chain capacity building
- Quality and reliability objectives

### 1.2 Reference Documents

- **MAAP1-REQ-001:** Program Requirements Baseline
- **MAAP1-IMS-001:** Integrated Master Schedule (IMS)
- **MAAP1-TECH-001:** Technical Baseline Document
- **MAAP1-SCM-001:** Supply Chain Management Plan
- **MAAP1-QMP-001:** Quality Management Plan
- **MAAP1-CFD-001:** Customer Demand Forecast

### 1.3 Production Philosophy

The ramp strategy employs a **risk-balanced approach** that:

1. Validates manufacturing processes at low rates before scaling
2. Incorporates lessons learned between phases
3. Stages capital investments to match rate requirements
4. Builds workforce capability progressively
5. Demonstrates quality and delivery performance before rate increases
6. Maintains flexibility for customer demand variations

## 4. Learning Curve Analysis

### 4.1 Learning Curve Assumptions

The MAAP-1 production cost model employs industry-standard learning curve theory with aerospace-specific parameters:

**Learning Rate:** 85% cumulative average  
**Basis:** Historical data from similar complex aerospace programs  
**Application:** Direct labor hours for airframe and integration activities

**Learning Curve Formula:**
```
Y = aX^b

Where:
Y = Average hours per unit for X units
a = Hours for first unit (theoretical)
X = Cumulative unit number
b = Learning curve slope = log(learning rate)/log(2)
```

|-------------|---------------------|------------|-----------------|
| 1 | 145,000 | 145,000 | 100% |

| 46 | 46,750 | 38,500 | 27% |
| 94 | 37,225 | 31,000 | 21% |
| 142 | 32,625 | 27,500 | 19% |

### 4.3 Learning Curve Considerations

**Factors Supporting Aggressive Learning:**
- Advanced digital manufacturing tools
- Significant automation investment
- Experienced workforce recruitment
- Supplier producibility improvements
- Lean manufacturing implementation

**Risk Factors to Learning Achievement:**
- Design changes during production
- Supply chain disruptions
- Workforce turnover
- Quality escapes requiring rework
- Pandemic or other external disruptions

**Mitigation:** Conservative learning curve applied (85% vs. optimistic 82%) with phase gates to validate assumptions before rate increases.

- Rotorcraft manufacturing (Sikorsky, Bell)

   - 2x rate increases maximum per transition

   - Supplier selection completed 24+ months before need
   - Capacity planning at contract award
   - Regular supplier health assessments

3. **Workforce Development Priority:**
   - Training infrastructure before hiring surge
   - Retention programs from program start
   - Clear career progression paths

4. **Digital Manufacturing Investment:**
   - Early adoption of Industry 4.0 technologies
   - Digital thread from design through production
   - Data-driven decision making

5. **Flexible Facility Design:**
   - Modular construction for scalability
   - Multi-program facility utilization
   - Reconfigurable production lines

- Post-unit build reviews (after each unit in LRIP)
