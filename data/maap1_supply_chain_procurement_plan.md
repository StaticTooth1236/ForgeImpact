**Introduction**

The Eurus Systems MAAP-1 program aims to design, develop, and deploy a microwave atmospheric absorption platform that provides high-resolution, real-time atmospheric sensing and communication capabilities. As part of the program, this Supply Chain Procurement Plan (SCP) outlines the procurement strategy, risk management, and logistical arrangements to ensure the timely and cost-effective procurement of materials and services required for the MAAP-1 platform. This plan is aligned with the Program Requirements Baseline (PRB), Key Specifications, and Bill of Materials (BOM).

**Scope**

This SCP applies to all variants of the MAAP-1 platform, including Firefighting, ISR, and Cargo variants. The plan covers the procurement of materials, components, and services required for the design, development, testing, and deployment of the MAAP-1 platform.

**Procurement Strategy**

The procurement strategy for the MAAP-1 program will be based on a combination of the following methods:

1. **Manufacturing**: Partner with established suppliers to manufacture components and subassemblies in-house or at supplier facilities.
2. **Procurement**: Purchase materials and components from qualified suppliers through a competitive bidding process.
3. **Development**: Collaborate with suppliers to develop new components, materials, or processes that meet the program's requirements.

**Risk Management**

The following risks will be addressed through the SCP:

1. **Component availability**: Ensure that critical components are available on schedule and in sufficient quantities.
2. **Supplier reliability**: Develop a robust supplier management process to ensure that suppliers meet their obligations.
3. **Intellectual property**: Protect Eurus Systems's intellectual property and ensure that suppliers do not infringe on it.
4. **Cybersecurity**: Implement measures to ensure the security of supplier data and prevent unauthorized access.

**Logistical Arrangements**

The following logistical arrangements will be implemented:

1. **Inventory management**: Establish an inventory management system to track materials and components.
2. **Shipping and transportation**: Arrange for timely and secure shipping of materials and components.
3. **Storage and warehousing**: Provide adequate storage and warehousing facilities for materials and components.

**Procurement Schedule**

The procurement schedule for the MAAP-1 program is outlined in the Integrated Master Schedule (IMS). The schedule is organized into five program phases: Conceptual Design (CD), Definition Design (DD), Development (D), Testing and Validation (TV), and Deployment (DPL). Each phase is further divided into schedule periods, with key milestones marked at the end of each period.

**Procurement Plan**

The following procurement plan will be implemented:

1. **Component procurement**: Procure components and materials in accordance with the BOM and Key Specifications.
2. **Subassembly procurement**: Procure subassemblies in accordance with the BOM and Key Specifications.
3. **Service procurement**: Procure services from qualified suppliers to support design, development, testing, and deployment activities.

**Procurement Methods**

The following procurement methods will be used:

1. **Competitive bidding**: Procure materials and components through a competitive bidding process.
2. **Request for proposal (RFP)**: Issue RFPs to suppliers to develop new components, materials, or processes.
3. **Request for quote (RFQ)**: Issue RFQs to suppliers for standard components and materials.

**Supplier Selection**

The following criteria will be used to select suppliers:

1. **Technical capability**: Ensure that suppliers have the technical capability to meet the program's requirements.
2. **Quality management**: Ensure that suppliers have a robust quality management system in place.
3. **Financial stability**: Ensure that suppliers have a stable financial position.
